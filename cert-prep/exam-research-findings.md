# GHL Admin Certification — Part B Research Findings

> You have already passed Part A. This document covers everything relevant to Part B.
> Last researched: April 2026

---

## Part B Structure

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Format | Live, on-camera, screen-sharing session with a HighLevel proctor |
| Time limit | 60 minutes maximum |
| Identity | Government-issued photo ID required at start |
| Task | Build a sub-account from scratch using a provided client brief |
| Attempts | 3 total; fail all 3 → must retake Part A at 95% before re-attempting Part B |
| Known scenario | Build a sub-account for a fictional client ("Doctor Bob" is the most frequently cited example) |
| Fastest recorded | 22 minutes |
| Average for passers | 32–45 minutes |

**Target:** Complete in under 30 minutes. This gives you a 2× buffer for the unexpected.

---

## Why ~51% Fail on First Attempt

In order of frequency:

1. **Navigation slowness** — Wasting time hunting for settings. The proctor watches you in real time. If you need 10 seconds to find every menu, that adds up to 15+ minutes of dead time over the session.
2. **Calendar integration** — Connecting Zoom or Google Meet to a calendar is confirmed as a Part B task. Many candidates have never done this in practice.
3. **Workflow not published** — Builds the workflow correctly but forgets to hit the "Publish" toggle before the proctor checks it.
4. **Wrong account level** — Clicking in Agency view when the task requires sub-account level, or vice versa. These two panels look similar and are easy to mix up under pressure.
5. **Time pressure / anxiety** — Candidates who knew the platform cold in practice take 3× longer on camera. The fix is deliberate timed drilling — not more studying.

## Certification Maintenance

- Badge validity: **2 years**
- Annual maintenance: Earn **2+ skills badges per year** to stay active
- Cert lapses if your HighLevel Agency subscription lapses
- If cert lapses > 90 days: may need to retake Part B via a third-party prep service

## Part B Scheduling Notes

- Proctor slots can be limited — book as early as possible after Part A
- Refresh the scheduling calendar regularly; slots appear and disappear quickly
- Community members sometimes swap slots — connect with the cert community if slots are full

---

## Tips from Certified Admins

**Speed drills (most important):**
- Practice building a sub-account from scratch under a 30-minute timer, repeatedly
- Do this until you can do it without thinking about *where* anything is
- The goal is automatic navigation, not just knowledge

**Specific things to drill:**
- Calendar → connect Zoom → test booking → verify Zoom link in confirmation
- Agency Settings vs Sub-Account Settings — know the difference without thinking
- Workflow: trigger → action → publish → test with a contact
- Snapshot: create from Agency → apply to a new sub-account

**On exam day:**
- Have a clean browser, good internet, ID ready
- Start with business profile and custom values (fast, builds confidence)
- Calendar integration second (the task most people fail — get it done while you're fresh)
- Workflow third
- Pipeline + contact last
- Take a snapshot at the end as your "save point"
